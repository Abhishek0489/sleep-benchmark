Quality Of Sleep

Quality of sleep refers to how well you sleep, not just how long. It includes falling asleep easily, staying asleep through the night, and feeling refreshed upon waking. Good sleep quality supports mental focus, emotional balance, and physical health. Poor quality sleep, even if long, can lead to fatigue, irritability, and health issues.

Stress Level

Stress level refers to the amount of mental or emotional strain a person is experiencing. It can be influenced by daily responsibilities, work pressure, personal challenges, or health issues. While a small amount of stress can be motivating, high or prolonged stress can affect sleep, concentration, mood, and overall well-being. Managing stress through relaxation, exercise, and healthy habits is important for maintaining balance and good health.

7/10

Sleep Duration

Sleep duration refers to the total amount of time a person spends sleeping each night. It plays a vital role in physical recovery, brain function, and emotional stability. While individual needs vary, most adults require 7 to 9 hours of sleep for optimal health. Too little or too much sleep can lead to fatigue, poor concentration, and increased risk of health problems. Maintaining a consistent sleep schedule helps improve both sleep duration and overall well-being.

7/10

BMI

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a numerical value calculated from a person’s height and weight. It is commonly used as a simple screening tool to categorize individuals into weight groups such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. While BMI gives a general indication of body fat, it does not measure fat directly or account for muscle mass, bone density, or distribution of fat. Despite its limitations, it remains a useful starting point for assessing health risks related to weight.

Blood Pressure

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against the walls of the arteries as the heart pumps. It is measured using two numbers: systolic (when the heart beats) over diastolic (when the heart rests between beats). A normal blood pressure is typically around 120/80 mmHg. Consistently high blood pressure, or hypertension, can lead to serious health problems like heart disease, stroke, and kidney damage. Monitoring and maintaining healthy blood pressure through diet, exercise, and stress management is essential for overall cardiovascular health.

Daily steps count how much you walk in a day. Taking around 8,000–10,000 steps daily boosts heart health, improves mood, helps manage weight, and lowers disease risk. Even small increases make a big difference..

Insomnia

Insomnia is a sleep disorder where a person struggles to fall or stay asleep. It can cause tiredness, poor focus, and mood issues. Common causes include stress, anxiety, or bad sleep habits. Treatment involves better sleep routines and stress management..
